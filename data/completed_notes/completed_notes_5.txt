<h1>Hyperbolic Identities, Derivatives, and Linear Approximations</h1>
<p style="color:whitesmoke;">
<strong>Hyperbolic identities</strong> provide relationships between fundamental hyperbolic functions, forming the basis for identities, simplifications, and derivations. Hyperbolic functions are analogues of trigonometric functions but use exponential definitions.
</p>
<!-- END_SECTION -->

<h3>Establishing Identities Involving Hyperbolic Functions</h3>
<p style="color:whitesmoke;">
The <strong>fundamental hyperbolic identity</strong> is:
</p>
<p style="color:whitesmoke;">
<code style="color:#00aaff; font-family: Menlo, Monaco, 'Courier New', monospace;">\cosh^2(x) - \sinh^2(x) = 1</code>
</p>
<p style="color:whitesmoke;">
This identity is analogous to the Pythagorean identity in trigonometry, but with subtraction instead of addition. Its importance lies in its ability to relate <strong>sinh</strong> and <strong>cosh</strong> and to construct or simplify other hyperbolic identities.
</p>
<!-- END_SECTION -->

<ol>
  <li>
    <p style="color:whitesmoke;">
      <strong>Verifying an identity involving <code style="color:#00aaff; font-family: Menlo, Monaco, 'Courier New', monospace;">\coth(x)</code>:</strong>
    </p>
    <p style="color:whitesmoke;">
      Given <code style="color:#00aaff; font-family: Menlo, Monaco, 'Courier New', monospace;">\coth(x) = \frac{\cosh(x)}{\sinh(x)}</code>, we can relate <strong>coth</strong> to the fundamental identity:
    </p>
    <ol>
      <li>
        <p style="color:whitesmoke;">
          Express <code style="color:#00aaff; font-family: Menlo, Monaco, 'Courier New', monospace;">\coth(x) = \frac{\cosh(x)}{\sinh(x)}</code>
        </p>
      </li>
      <li>
        <p style="color:whitesmoke;">
          Note that <code style="color:#00aaff; font-family: Menlo, Monaco, 'Courier New', monospace;">\cosh^2(x) - \sinh^2(x) = 1 \implies \cosh^2(x) = 1 + \sinh^2(x)</code>
        </p>
      </li>
      <li>
        <p style="color:whitesmoke;">
          Hence, <code style="color:#00aaff; font-family: Menlo, Monaco, 'Courier New', monospace;">\coth^2(x) = \frac{\cosh^2(x)}{\sinh^2(x)} = \frac{1 + \sinh^2(x)}{\sinh^2(x)}</code>
        </p>
      </li>
      <li>
        <p style="color:whitesmoke;">
          Simplifying: <code style="color:#00aaff; font-family: Menlo, Monaco, 'Courier New', monospace;">\coth^2(x) = 1 + \frac{1}{\sinh^2(x)} = 1 + \csch^2(x)</code>
        </p>
      </li>
    </ol>
    <p style="color:whitesmoke;">
      Therefore, one verified identity is <code style="color:#00aaff; font-family: Menlo, Monaco, 'Courier New', monospace;">\coth^2(x) - 1 = \csch^2(x)</code>.
    </p>
  </li>
  <li>
    <p style="color:whitesmoke;">
      <strong>Verifying <code style="color:#00aaff; font-family: Menlo, Monaco, 'Courier New', monospace;">\tanh^2(x) + \sech^2(x) = 1</code> using the fundamental identity</strong>:
    </p>
    <ol>
      <li>
        <p style="color:whitesmoke;">
          Recall the definitions:
        </p>
        <ul>
          <li><p style="color:whitesmoke;"><code style="color:#00aaff; font-family: Menlo, Monaco, 'Courier New', monospace;">\tanh(x) = \frac{\sinh(x)}{\cosh(x)}</code></p></li>
          <li><p style="color:whitesmoke;"><code style="color:#00aaff; font-family: Menlo, Monaco, 'Courier New', monospace;">\sech(x) = \frac{1}{\cosh(x)}</code></p></li>
        </ul>
      </li>
      <li>
        <p style="color:whitesmoke;">
          Compute <code style="color:#00aaff; font-family: Menlo, Monaco, 'Courier New', monospace;">\tanh^2(x) + \sech^2(x) = \left(\frac{\sinh(x)}{\cosh(x)}\right)^2 + \left(\frac{1}{\cosh(x)}\right)^2 = \frac{\sinh^2(x) + 1}{\cosh^2(x)}</code>
        </p>
      </li>
      <li>
        <p style="color:whitesmoke;">
          From the fundamental identity, <code style="color:#00aaff; font-family: Menlo, Monaco, 'Courier New', monospace;">1 = \cosh^2(x) - \sinh^2(x) \implies \cosh^2(x) = 1 + \sinh^2(x)</code>
        </p>
      </li>
      <li>
        <p style="color:whitesmoke;">
          Therefore, <code style="color:#00aaff; font-family: Menlo, Monaco, 'Courier New', monospace;">\frac{\sinh^2(x) + 1}{\cosh^2(x)} = \frac{\cosh^2(x)}{\cosh^2(x)} = 1</code>
        </p>
      </li>
    </ol>
    <p style="color:whitesmoke;">
      Thus the verified identity: <code style="color:#00aaff; font-family: Menlo, Monaco, 'Courier New', monospace;">\tanh^2(x) + \sech^2(x) = 1</code>.
    </p>
  </li>
  <li>
    <p style="color:whitesmoke;">
      <strong>Deriving the formula for the derivative of <code style="color:#00aaff; font-family: Menlo, Monaco, 'Courier New', monospace;">\coth(x)</code>:</strong>
    </p>
    <ol>
      <li>
        <p style="color:whitesmoke;">
          Given <code style="color:#00aaff; font-family: Menlo, Monaco, 'Courier New', monospace;">\coth(x) = \frac{\cosh(x)}{\sinh(x)}</code>, apply the quotient rule:
        </p>
        <p style="color:whitesmoke;">
          <code style="color:#00aaff; font-family: Menlo, Monaco, 'Courier New', monospace;">
          \frac{d}{dx} \coth(x) = \frac{\sinh(x) \cdot \sinh(x) - \cosh(x) \cdot \cosh(x)}{\sinh^2(x)}
          </code>
        </p>
      </li>
      <li>
        <p style="color:whitesmoke;">
          This numerator simplifies as:
        </p>
        <p style="color:whitesmoke;">
          <code style="color:#00aaff; font-family: Menlo, Monaco, 'Courier New', monospace;">
            \sinh^2(x) - \cosh^2(x) = -(\cosh^2(x) - \sinh^2(x))
          </code>
        </p>
      </li>
      <li>
        <p style="color:whitesmoke;">
          By the fundamental identity, <code style="color:#00aaff; font-family: Menlo, Monaco, 'Courier New', monospace;">\cosh^2(x) - \sinh^2(x) = 1</code>, so the derivative becomes:
        </p>
        <p style="color:whitesmoke;">
          <code style="color:#00aaff; font-family: Menlo, Monaco, 'Courier New', monospace;">
          \frac{d}{dx} \coth(x) = -\frac{1}{\sinh^2(x)} = -\csch^2(x)
          </code>
        </p>
      </li>
    </ol>
    <p style="color:whitesmoke;">
      So, the derivative is <code style="color:#00aaff; font-family: Menlo, Monaco, 'Courier New', monospace;">\frac{d}{dx} \coth(x) = -\csch^2(x)</code>.
    </p>
  </li>
</ol>

<h3>Linearization and Differentials with Approximations</h3>
<p style="color:whitesmoke;">
Linearization is a method that uses the tangent line to a function at a given point to approximate the function's value nearby. The <strong>linearization</strong> <code style="color:#00aaff; font-family: Menlo, Monaco, 'Courier New', monospace;">L(x)</code> of a function <code style="color:#00aaff; font-family: Menlo, Monaco, 'Courier New', monospace;">f(x)</code> at <code style="color:#00aaff; font-family: Menlo, Monaco, 'Courier New', monospace;">x=a</code> is given by:
</p>
<p style="color:whitesmoke;">
<code style="color:#00aaff; font-family: Menlo, Monaco, 'Courier New', monospace;">
L(x) = f(a) + f'(a)(x - a)
</code>
</p>
<p style="color:whitesmoke;">
This formula approximates <strong>f(x)</strong> near <strong>x = a</strong> using the tangent line at that point. The method is a direct application of the equation of a tangent line in calculus and is important for estimating function values where direct evaluation may be difficult.
</p>
<!-- END_SECTION -->

<ol>
  <li>
    <p style="color:whitesmoke;">
      <strong>Linearization Example</strong>: Find the linearization <code style="color:#00aaff; font-family: Menlo, Monaco, 'Courier New', monospace;">L(x)</code> of <code style="color:#00aaff; font-family: Menlo, Monaco, 'Courier New', monospace;">f(x) = 3x^2</code> at <code style="color:#00aaff; font-family: Menlo, Monaco, 'Courier New', monospace;">x=a</code>.
    </p>
    <ol>
      <li>
        <p style="color:whitesmoke;">
          Compute <code style="color:#00aaff; font-family: Menlo, Monaco, 'Courier New', monospace;">f'(x) = 6x</code>.
        </p>
      </li>
      <li>
        <p style="color:whitesmoke;">
          Substitute into the linearization formula:<br>
          <code style="color:#00aaff; font-family: Menlo, Monaco, 'Courier New', monospace;">L(x) = 3a^2 + 6a(x-a)</code>
        </p>
      </li>
    </ol>
    <p style="color:whitesmoke;">
      This provides a linear estimate for <code style="color:#00aaff; font-family: Menlo, Monaco, 'Courier New', monospace;">f(x)</code> near <code style="color:#00aaff; font-family: Menlo, Monaco, 'Courier New', monospace;">x=a</code>.
    </p>
  </li>
  <li>
    <p style="color:whitesmoke;">
      <strong>Relationship between small changes in <code style="color:#00aaff; font-family: Menlo, Monaco, 'Courier New', monospace;">x</code> and <code style="color:#00aaff; font-family: Menlo, Monaco, 'Courier New', monospace;">y</code>:</strong>
    </p>
    <p style="color:whitesmoke;">
      The <strong>differential</strong> expresses how a small change in the input (<code style="color:#00aaff; font-family: Menlo, Monaco, 'Courier New', monospace;">dx</code>) leads to a small change in the output (<code style="color:#00aaff; font-family: Menlo, Monaco, 'Courier New', monospace;">dy</code>), using the approximation:
    </p>
    <p style="color:whitesmoke;">
      <code style="color:#00aaff; font-family: Menlo, Monaco, 'Courier New', monospace;">dy \approx f'(x) \, dx</code>
    </p>
    <p style="color:whitesmoke;">
      This relationship is crucial for error estimation and applied problems in calculus and analysis.
    </p>
  </li>
  <li>
    <p style="color:whitesmoke;">
      <strong>Using Differentials for Numerical Approximation</strong>:
    </p>
    <ol>
      <li>
        <p style="color:whitesmoke;">
          Identify the function and point of approximation, e.g., <code style="color:#00aaff; font-family: Menlo, Monaco, 'Courier New', monospace;">y = f(x)</code> at <code style="color:#00aaff; font-family: Menlo, Monaco, 'Courier New', monospace;">x = a</code>.
        </p>
      </li>
      <li>
        <p style="color:whitesmoke;">
          Compute the derivative <code style="color:#00aaff; font-family: Menlo, Monaco, 'Courier New', monospace;">f'(a)</code>.
        </p>
      </li>
      <li>
        <p style="color:whitesmoke;">
          Use <code style="color:#00aaff; font-family: Menlo, Monaco, 'Courier New', monospace;">dy \approx f'(a)\,dx</code> to approximate small changes, where <code style="color:#00aaff; font-family: Menlo, Monaco, 'Courier New', monospace;">dx = x - a</code>.
        </p>
      </li>
    </ol>
    <p style="color:whitesmoke;">
      For example, to estimate <code style="color:#00aaff; font-family: Menlo, Monaco, 'Courier New', monospace;">f(1.07)</code> using <code style="color:#00aaff; font-family: Menlo, Monaco, 'Courier New', monospace;">\ln(1.07)</code>, linearize at <code style="color:#00aaff; font-family: Menlo, Monaco, 'Courier New', monospace;">x=1</code>, compute <code style="color:#00aaff; font-family: Menlo, Monaco, 'Courier New', monospace;">f'(1) = 1</code>, and approximate <code style="color:#00aaff; font-family: Menlo, Monaco, 'Courier New', monospace;">dy \approx 1 \times 0.07 = 0.07</code>.
    </p>
  </li>
</ol>
